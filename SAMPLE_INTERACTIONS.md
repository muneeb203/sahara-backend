## Example 1: Inheritance Rights

**User Query:**
> "What are my inheritance rights as a daughter in Pakistan?"

**HerHaq Response:**
> As a daughter in Pakistan, you have clear inheritance rights under both Islamic law and Pakistani legislation. According to Islamic inheritance law, a daughter is entitled to inherit from her father's estate. The specific share depends on the presence of other heirs.
>
> Under the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961, Section 4, if your father passes away, you are entitled to receive a share of his property. In the presence of sons, daughters receive half the share that sons receive. If there are only daughters and no sons, they collectively receive two-thirds of the estate if there are two or more daughters, or half if there is only one daughter.
>
> The Quran explicitly grants women the right to inherit, and this right cannot be denied. If you face any obstacles in claiming your inheritance, you can seek legal assistance through the courts.
>
> **Sources:**
> 1. **Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961** (Section 4)
> 2. **Sahih Muslim** - Hadith on inheritance rights
> 3. **Case Study**: Islamic Inheritance Rights for Women in Pakistan (2025)
>

## Example 2: Workplace Harassment

**User Query:**
> "My boss keeps making inappropriate comments. How can I file a harassment complaint?"

**HerHaq Response:**
> I'm sorry you're experiencing this. You have legal protection under Pakistani law, and there are clear steps you can take.
>
> According to the Protection Against Harassment of Women at the Workplace Act, 2010, you have the right to file a formal complaint. Here's what you can do:
>
> **Step 1: Internal Complaint**
> Your organization must have an Inquiry Committee (Section 3). This committee must include at least one woman member. You can file a written complaint with this committee within 3 months of the incident.
>
> **Step 2: Inquiry Process**
> The committee will investigate your complaint within 30 days. They will hear both sides and examine evidence. You have the right to be heard and present witnesses.
>
> **Step 3: If Internal Process Fails**
> If you're not satisfied with the internal inquiry or if your organization doesn't have a committee, you can file a complaint with the Federal or Provincial Ombudsperson within 30 days.
>
> **Important Rights:**
> - Your complaint will be kept confidential
> - You cannot be retaliated against for filing a complaint
> - If you're in trauma, your organization must arrange counseling (Section 4(7))
> - False accusations can result in penalties
>
> **Penalties for Harassment:**
> If found guilty, the harasser can face: censure, withholding of promotion, compulsory retirement, removal from service, or dismissal (Section 4(4)).
>
> **Sources:**
> 1. **Protection Against Harassment of Women at the Workplace Act, 2010** (Sections 2, 3, 4, 7)
> 2. **Case Study**: Workplace Harassment Cases in Pakistan
>
> *Note: This information is for educational purposes. For legal matters, please consult a qualified lawyer.*

## Example 3: Polygamy

**User Query:**
> "Can my husband marry another woman without telling me?"

**HerHaq Response:**
> No, your husband cannot legally marry another woman without following proper procedures under Pakistani law.
>
> According to the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961, Section 6:
>
> **Legal Requirements:**
> 1. Your husband must obtain written permission from an Arbitration Council before contracting another marriage
> 2. He must submit an application stating reasons for the proposed marriage
> 3. He must state whether he has obtained your consent
> 4. The Arbitration Council will decide if the proposed marriage is "necessary and just"
>
> **Your Rights:**
> - You have the right to be informed and consulted
> - You can nominate a representative to the Arbitration Council
> - You can object to the proposed marriage
> - The Council must consider your position
>
> **Consequences of Violation:**
> If your husband marries without permission:
> 1. He must immediately pay the entire dower (mahr) - both prompt and deferred amounts
> 2. He can face imprisonment up to one year
> 3. He can be fined up to Rs. 5,000
> 4. Or both imprisonment and fine
>
> **Islamic Perspective:**
> While Islam permits polygamy under specific conditions, it emphasizes justice and equal treatment. The Quran states that if a man cannot treat wives equally, he should marry only one.
>
> **What You Can Do:**
> - If he has already married without permission, you can file a legal complaint
> - You can claim your full dower immediately
> - You can seek divorce if you choose
> - Consult a family lawyer for your specific situation
>
> **Sources:**
> 1. **Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961** (Section 6)
> 2. **Sahih Muslim** - Hadith on justice in marriage
> 3. **Case Study**: Polygamy cases in Pakistani courts
>

## Example 4: Divorce Rights

**User Query:**
> "My husband said 'talaq' three times. Am I divorced immediately?"

**HerHaq Response:**
> No, you are not divorced immediately. Pakistani law provides a waiting period and reconciliation process.
>
> According to the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961, Section 7:
>
> **Legal Process:**
> 1. Your husband must give written notice to the Chairman of the Union Council immediately after pronouncing talaq
> 2. He must also provide you with a copy of this notice
> 3. The talaq is NOT effective immediately
> 4. There is a 90-day waiting period from the date the notice is delivered to the Chairman
> 5. During this period, an Arbitration Council will be formed to attempt reconciliation
>
> **Important Points:**
> - If you are pregnant, the talaq is not effective until 90 days OR the end of pregnancy, whichever is later
> - The talaq can be revoked during the 90-day period
> - If your husband did not give proper notice, he can face imprisonment up to one year or a fine up to Rs. 5,000
>
> **Triple Talaq:**
> The practice of instant triple talaq (saying "talaq" three times at once) is not recognized as immediate divorce under Pakistani law. The same 90-day process applies.
>
> **Your Rights:**
> - You have the right to participate in the reconciliation process
> - You can nominate a representative to the Arbitration Council
> - You are entitled to your dower (mahr) and maintenance during the waiting period
> - After divorce, you are entitled to maintenance during iddat (waiting period)
>
> **What You Should Do:**
> 1. Verify if proper notice was given to the Union Council
> 2. Participate in the reconciliation process if you wish
> 3. Consult a lawyer to understand your financial rights
> 4. Keep all documentation
>
> **Sources:**
> 1. **Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961** (Section 7)
> 2. **Case Study**: Divorce procedures in Pakistan
> 3. **Sahih Muslim** - Hadith on divorce procedures
>
> *Note: This information is for educational purposes. For legal matters, please consult a qualified lawyer.*

## Example 5: Property Rights

**User Query:**
> "Can I buy property in my own name?"

**HerHaq Response:**
> Yes, absolutely! Women in Pakistan have full legal rights to own, buy, sell, and manage property in their own name.
>
> **Your Rights:**
> 1. You can purchase property independently without anyone's permission
> 2. You can register property in your name
> 3. You can sell or transfer property as you wish
> 4. You can inherit property from your parents and other relatives
> 5. You have the right to your mahr (dower) as property
>
> **Islamic Perspective:**
> Islam grants women complete economic independence. The Quran explicitly states that women have the right to own and manage their wealth. Your property remains yours even after marriage - your husband has no automatic right to it.
>
> According to authentic ahadith, women during the time of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) owned property, engaged in business, and had full control over their wealth.
>
> **Legal Framework:**
> Pakistani law fully supports women's property rights. The Constitution of Pakistan guarantees equal rights to all citizens. Various laws protect women's right to:
> - Inherit property (Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961)
> - Own agricultural land
> - Own commercial property
> - Own residential property
>
> **Practical Steps:**
> 1. You can directly approach property dealers or sellers
> 2. Conduct proper legal verification of the property
> 3. Register the property in your name at the relevant authority
> 4. Keep all documentation safe
>
> **Common Misconceptions:**
> - You do NOT need your husband's or father's permission
> - You do NOT need a male guardian to buy property
> - Your property does NOT automatically transfer to your husband
> - You CAN manage your property independently
>
> **Sources:**
> 1. **Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961** (Property rights)
> 2. **Sahih Muslim** - Hadith on women's economic rights
> 3. **Case Study**: Women's property rights in Pakistan
>
> *Note: This information is for educational purposes. For legal matters, please consult a qualified lawyer.*
